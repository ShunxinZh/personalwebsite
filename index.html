Current Inquiry 2026

Transcription Factor Regulatory Divergence

Researching how homologous transcription factors diverge in regulatory function across species. Using interspecies yeast hybrids, I study the molecular basis of regulatory evolution and gene expression divergence.

Nodule Senescence Regulation Network

Examining how transcription factors control soybean nodule senescence through coordinated gene regulatory networks. By identifying key regulators and their downstream pathways, I aim to better understand nodule longevity and improve symbiotic nitrogen fixation efficiency in soybean.

Exploring how evolution rewires gene regulation to generate biological diversity.

Currently a Graduate Student in Biological Sciences at Purdue University. My research asks how homologous transcription factors evolve different regulatory programs despite pleiotropic constraint. Using Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Saccharomyces eubayanus in a hybrid yeast system, I combine reciprocal hemizygosity , promoter swapping, and RNA-seq to quantify how often transcription factors diverge and whether divergence is driven mainly by changes within promoters or coding regions. I also test the extent to which differences in regulation change across environments and use ancestral reconstruction to determine how regulatory divergence accumulates across lineages. My long-term research interest lies in uncovering how gene regulatory systems organize biological processes and drive functional adaptation across diverse living systems.
